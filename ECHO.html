ECHO


  • Introduction

During a 48-hour game jam organized by Games Inkubator at Kingston University, our team developed ECHO, a medieval escape room-style game centered around the theme "Everything Falling Apart." In the game, players explore a forbidden castle and, after interacting with a mysterious painting, are transported through a time portal, trapping them in another era. As the character navigates the room, the light gradually fades, making it increasingly difficult to find the broken pieces of the painting needed to escape.

  • Project Title: ECHO - Game Jam Project
  • Role: Lead Programmer / UI Designer
  • Team Size: 4 (2 Programmers, 1 Designer, 1 Artist)
  • Platform: PC
  • Technologies Used: Unity, C#, Maya, Adobe Illustrator

My Contributions

    As the lead programmer, I was responsible for coding the game mechanics in C#. I focused on the lighting system, which was crucial to the gameplay—gradually dimming the light to increase the challenge as players raced against time. This feature was integral to maintaining the tension and atmosphere that our game designer envisioned. I also added a camera shake effect to enhance the game's immersion during critical moments, making the experience more dynamic. Additionally, I designed the game’s UI using Adobe Illustrator, ensuring it was intuitive and aligned with the medieval theme. The animations were created in Maya, which added a polished, professional feel to the game. Working on ECHO was an excellent opportunity to combine my programming and design skills, while collaborating closely with a talented team under tight deadlines.

What I learned

This game jam provided valuable lessons in both teamwork and technical execution. Collaborating in a high-pressure, time-constrained environment reinforced the importance of clear communication and staying aligned with the team’s creative vision. Coding the fading light mechanics and integrating other effects in C# taught me a lot about balancing gameplay elements to create a cohesive and immersive experience. Designing the UI in Adobe Illustrator and working with Maya for animations gave me deeper insights into how different tools and techniques come together in game development. Overall, this project strengthened my ability to adapt quickly, think creatively, and deliver quality work under pressure.
